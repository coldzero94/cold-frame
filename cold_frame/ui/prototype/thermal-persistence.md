# Thermal Persistence

*An algorithmic philosophy for the Coldframe memory field.*

## The movement

**Thermal Persistence** is the study of warmth held against entropy. A memory is not a
record sitting inertly in a row; it is a small fire. Left alone, every fire cools — this
is the second law, and it is also forgetting. What the field renders is not data but
*temperature*: the residual heat each belief still carries, the rate at which it is losing
that heat to the dark, and the rare few that someone chose to shelter under glass so the
cold could not reach them. The image is never a chart. It is a hearth at night, seen from
above, where the bright coals are what you still believe and the grey ash is what you are
letting go. Beauty here is not decoration; it is the honest visualization of an invisible
quantity — retained conviction — made legible as light.

The philosophy is fundamentally **conservative of place**. Each ember is born at a position
fixed forever by the identity of its memory — a deterministic coordinate hashed from the
note's stable id, jittered by a master-tuned blue-noise relaxation so the field breathes
without ever clustering or colliding. Nothing in this world is allowed to move. When a
belief strengthens or fades, it does not drift to a new home; it only grows hotter or
cooler, brighter or dimmer, swells or contracts in place. This is the one inviolable law,
and it is what makes the field a *map of a person* rather than a churning screensaver: the
eye learns where things live, and spatial memory does the rest. A meticulously crafted
implementation will honor this constraint so completely that a returning viewer recognizes
the constellation of their own mind before reading a single word.

Heat is the soul of the system, and heat is layered. Each ember holds a **core** — a small,
fierce point whose color temperature is driven by belief strength: the warmest convictions
burn toward white-gold, the middling ones glow a steady ember-orange, and the cooling ones
slide down through dim copper into the slate-blue of near-ash. Around the core lives a
**bloom** — a soft radial falloff, a halo of escaping warmth whose radius and opacity scale
with strength, rendered through painstakingly tuned additive layering so that neighboring
glows pool into a luminous fog exactly the way coals share heat in a banked fire. The
falloff curve is not a naive linear gradient; it is an easing refined over countless
iterations until the light feels physical, radiometric, the product of someone who has
studied how warmth actually leaves a body.

Stillness would be a lie, because heat is never still. A continuous **thermal turbulence** —
layered Perlin noise sampled in space and slow time — perturbs every bloom, so the field
shimmers like air rising off coals. Crucially, the amplitude of an ember's shimmer is not
uniform: it is coupled to that memory's *retrievability*, the live decay term. Confident,
freshly-touched beliefs sit steady and certain; the faltering ones — low confidence, long
untouched, the ones the system flags as **at risk** — flicker and gutter like a flame
starved of fuel, a frost-blue tremor crawling at their edges. This coupling of noise
amplitude to data is where the algorithm earns its keep: turbulence is not garnish, it is a
second, pre-attentive channel of meaning, choreographed with the deep computational
expertise of a master who knows that the eye reads *motion* before it reads *brightness*.

And then there is the glass. A handful of memories have been deliberately **pinned** —
sheltered, in the gardener's sense, under a cold frame whose transparent lid traps the day's
warmth so the seedling survives the freezing night. These the algorithm must crown with a
faint, faceted enclosure: a thin crystalline boundary, caught light along its edges,
geometric where everything else is soft. The pinned embers never cool no matter how the
field decays around them; they are the overwintered few, and the master-level rendering
should make their protection *felt* — a quiet structural calm amid the organic flicker, so
subtle that only a careful eye names it, yet so essential that the whole composition would
feel exposed without it.

Every run is a single banked fire reaching equilibrium, not an animation looping toward
nothing. The seed does not change *which* memories exist or *how hot* they are — that truth
is fixed by the data — but it re-rolls the field's spatial relaxation and the phase of the
turbulence, so each seed is another honest photograph of the same mind on the same night,
from a slightly different angle. The implementation that results should look like it cost
weeks of painstaking optimization: thermal falloff tuned to the photon, blue-noise
placement refined until it looks inevitable, turbulence calibrated until the at-risk embers
gutter with real anxiety and the evergreen ones hold with real certainty. This is controlled
chaos in service of a single, sober idea — that what we choose to keep warm is what we
remember, and that forgetting, rendered with enough care, is beautiful.
